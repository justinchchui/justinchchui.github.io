Atomic Heat Conduction Simulator

Observe the difference between heat transfer via lattice vibrations (non-metals) and the rapid transfer enabled by a "sea of free electrons" (metals).

Non-Metal

Heat moves slowly. Atoms vibrate and pass energy only to their immediate neighbors.

Metal

Heat moves rapidly. Fast-moving free electrons carry thermal energy directly across the material.

What's happening? When you apply heat to the left edge, the leftmost atoms start to vibrate heavily (turning red). In the non-metal, they can only pass this energy by bumping into their fixed neighbors—a slow process. In the metal, notice the tiny yellow/red sparks (free electrons). They collide with the hot atoms, absorb the heat energy, and instantly bounce across the lattice, colliding with cold atoms on the far side and heating them up almost immediately!
