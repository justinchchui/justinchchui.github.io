Convex Lens (Converging)

Bends parallel light rays inwards to a focal point. Can form both real (inverted) and virtual (upright) images.

Concave Lens (Diverging)

Spreads parallel light rays outwards. Always forms a virtual, upright, and smaller image.

What's happening?

We find the image by drawing two primary rays from the tip of the object:

  • Parallel Ray (Top): Travels horizontally to the lens. A convex lens bends it through the far focal point. A concave lens bends it away from the near focal point.
  • Central Ray (Middle): Passes straight through the optical center (0,0) of the lens without bending.

Where these rays (or their virtual dashed extensions) intersect is where the tip of the image is formed. If the image forms on the side light is traveling to, it is Real. If traced backward, it is Virtual.
