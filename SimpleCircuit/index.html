Physical Circuit Simulator

A mechanical "marble run" analogy for electrical circuits. The Battery is an elevator pumping charges to a high potential energy. Light Bulbs are sloped ramps filled with obstacles (atoms) that restrict flow. Adjust the Resistance to change the size of the obstacles! Repulsion: Notice how charges naturally space out and create "traffic jams" at the bulbs because they repel each other.

Left Click: Rotate | Right Click: Pan | Scroll: Zoom

Series Circuit Physics

Notice the two distinct "stairs". Because the total height (voltage) must be shared across both bulbs, each drop is smaller. Because charges repel each other, a marble slowing down at the bulb pushes backward on the ones behind it. This creates a cascading traffic jam that restricts the flow rate (current) throughout the entire path!

Parallel Circuit Physics

The path smoothly splits into two branches. Notice that both ramps fall the entire height of the elevator. Since both paths get the full voltage drop, both bulbs can glow brightly. Because the charges have two separate avenues to go down, they don't bunch up as severely, allowing double the overall flow rate to be drawn from the battery!
