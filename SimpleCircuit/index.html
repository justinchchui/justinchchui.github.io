Physical Circuit Simulator

A mechanical "marble run" analogy for electrical circuits. The Battery is an elevator pumping charges to a high potential energy. Light Bulbs are sloped ramps filled with obstacles (atoms) that restrict flow. Repulsion: Notice how charges naturally space out. Turn the switch OFF to see the track (potential) drop to zero!

Left Click: Rotate | Right Click: Pan | Scroll: Zoom

Series Circuit Physics

Notice the two distinct "stairs". Because the total height (voltage) must be shared across both bulbs, each drop is smaller. Because charges repel each other, a marble slowing down at the bulb pushes backward on the ones behind it. This creates a cascading traffic jam that restricts the flow rate (current) throughout the entire path!

Parallel Circuit Physics

The path smoothly splits into two branches. Notice that both ramps fall the entire height of the elevator. Since both paths get the full voltage drop, both bulbs can glow brightly. Also, observe how the charges dynamically route themselves: more charges flow through the branch with lower resistance, visually demonstrating how current favors the path of least resistance!
