Conservation of Momentum

Watch the Σ Momentum readout. No matter the collision type, the total momentum before perfectly matches the total momentum after.

Kinetic Energy (KE)

KE = ½mv². In Elastic collisions, Total KE is conserved. In Inelastic collisions, some KE is lost to heat/sound. In Perfectly Inelastic collisions, maximum KE is lost!

Coefficient of Restitution

This determines "bounciness" (e). Elastic has e=1 (perfect bounce). Inelastic here uses e=0.5. Perfectly Inelastic uses e=0 (they stick together).
